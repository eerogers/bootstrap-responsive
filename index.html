About Me

Elizabeth started coding in 2018 with The Coding Boot Camp at UCI's Continuing Education Division; she is eager to create content using her newly-acquired skills.

She has been living in Southern California since May 2017. Other places she’s lived include Boone, North Carolina; New York, New York; New Haven, Connecticut; Normal, Illinois; Saint Petersburg, Russia; and Omaha, Nebraska, where she grew up.

She has master’s degrees from Yale University and Columbia University, in European and Russian Studies and in Anthropology, respectively. Her undergraduate degree is in International Studies, from the University of Nebraska at Omaha.

Ultimately, she hopes to earn a doctorate in Anthropology. She also hopes to write a novel, design her own app, and travel to Reykjavik, Iceland.

In addition to English, Elizabeth speaks French and Russian and she is trying to learn Japanese. She loves true crime, tv, video games, and dogs. She has a French bulldog named Aster.
